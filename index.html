Sustainable Developement Goals


The SDGs are 17 linked goals, which act as a base for 'a better and sustainable future.'


The SDGs were set up by UN-GA in 2015, and these goals were to be achieved by 2030.


The SDGs were made to succeed the Millennium Developement Goals which ended in 2015.


The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015,


Provides a shared blueprint for peace and prosperity for people and the planet, now and into the future.
